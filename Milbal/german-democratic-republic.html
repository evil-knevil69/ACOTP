GERMAN DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC Population: 17,285,000. Military service: 18 months. Total regular forces: 131,000. Estimated GNP 1971 : $35.9 billion. Defence budget 1972: 7,600 million Ostmarks (£2,240,000,000). 3.39 Ostmarks = $1.

Army: 90,000.

2 tank divisions. 4 motorized rifle divisions. 12 Some heavy tanks; about 2,000 medium tanks, T-54, T-55, and T-62; several hundred T-34 (used for training); about 130 PT-76 It tanks; BRDM scout cars; BTR-50P, BTR-60P and BTR-152 armoured personnel carrier; SU-100 self-propelled guns; 85mm, 122mm, 130mm and 152mm guns; FROG 1 and Scud B surface-to-surface missile; 57mm and 100mm anti-tank guns; Sagger, Snapper and Swatter anti-tank guided weapon; 23mm and 57mm self-propelled AA guns; and 100mm A A guns.

Navy: 16,000.

2 destroyer escorts. 25 coastal escorts. 49 minesweepers. 12 O^a-class patrol boats with Styx surface-to-surface missile. 65 motor torpedo boats (45 less than 100 tons). 18 landing ships and craft. 16 Mi-4 helicopters.

Air Force: 25,000; 304 combat aircraft.

2 interceptor squadrons with MiG- 17. 17 interceptor squadrons with MiG-21. (16 aircraft in a combat squadron.) 30 transports, including An-2 and Il-14. 30 Mi-1, Mi-4 and Mi-8 helicopters. An AD division of 9,000 (5 regiments), with about 120 57mm and 100mm AA guns and SA-2 surface-to-air missile.

Para-Military Forces: 60,000.

40,000 Border Guards (Grenzschutztruppe) includ¬ ing a Border Command separate from the regular army. 20,000 security troops plus 350,000 in armed workers’ organizations (Kampfgruppen der Arbeiterklasse).
