FINLAND Population: 4,650,000. Military service : 8-1 1 months. but for purposes of comparison have been listed separately. t The Irish £ has been allowed to fluctuate since 22 June 1972. 26 Total armed forces: 39,500. Estimated GNP 1971: $11.2 billion. Defence budget 1972: 747.8 million markkaa ($182 million). 4.2 markkaa=$1 1 July 1971. 4.1 markkaa = $1 1 July 1972.

Army: 34,000.

1 armoured brigade (at about half strength). 6 infantry brigades (at about 35 per cent strength). 6 independent infantry battalions. 6 coastal artillery battalions. 4 anti-aircraft battalions. T-54, T-55 and Charioteer medium tanks; PT-76 It tanks; BTR-50P armoured personnel carrier; 105mm, 122mm and 130mm guns; 122mm and 152mm how; Vigilant and SS-11 anti-tank guided weapon; ZSU 57-2 35mm, Oerlikon and 40mm Bofors AA guns.

Navy: 2,500.

3 frigates (one used as training ship). 2 gunboats. 1 patrol boat with surface-to-surface missile. 15 fast patrol boats (less than 100 tons). 2 coastal minelayers. 5 inshore minesweepers.

Air Force: 3,000 ; 47 combat aircraft.

3 fighter squadrons with MiG-21 F and Gnat Mark 1 (12 Saab J-35BS Draken will replace Gnats by 1974). About 100 trainers, incl 60 M agister, 30 Safir and a few MiG-15/21 UTI. About 10 DC-3 and Beaver transports. Mi-4, Alouette II and 4 AB-204B helicopters.

reserves: 650,000.

Para- Military Forces: 4,000 Frontier-defence guards.

* Austrian air units are an integral part of the Army
